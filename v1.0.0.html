01

Overview

A third-person, superhero-style flight battle through the astral plane, alongside a mysterious and ancient emissary. Built for PC on Unreal Engine 5.8, Empyrean Rifts fuses the exhilarating freedom of flight with gravity-based abilities. This demo is a slice of the ultimate powerset — it introduces the game’s “big bad,” the Vampire King, while withholding the protagonist’s ultimate secret—their identity as the Galaxy Soul incarnate—as the hook for the full game.

02

Story

The Soul Sphere—an ancient cosmic artifact binding all realms—rests in a crystalline structure in the Astral Plane, guarded by astral beings. The Vampire King, disguised as an emissary of the desperate, warns of an impending invasion and tricks the player into destroying the Sphere.

The shattering scatters fragments across realms, tearing open rifts in the fabric between worlds — rough-edged wounds that serve as windows and gateways. Through one, a purple-sky mushroom realm; another bleeds fiery light from Tartarus. The player must recover the fragments, repair the Sphere, and banish the Vampire King — discovering, along the way, that they are the soul of the galaxy incarnate.

Demo Arc

The player enters the Astral Plane, meets the emissary, and flies through soul-currents fighting waves of astral beings with innate gravity powers — then destroys the portal to the Earth Realm. The demo closes as the Vampire King reveals his true plan: using you to shatter the Soul Sphere and free himself from Tartarus.

03

Player Mechanics

Movement

Flight

Superhero flight: left stick / WASD for horizontal movement, space & shift (or bumpers) for elevation, and right stick / mouse for camera aiming.

Ranged Attack

Kinetic Punch

Punch a blast of kinetic force in the direction you're aiming, hurling enemies and debris away from you.

Area of Effect

Singularity

Create a single point of gravity that pulls enemies and objects inward — imploding anything that reaches the center before the event ends.

04

Core Gameplay Loop

I

Fight

Battle waves of astral beings across the soul-currents.

II

Destroy

Tear open and destroy the portal to the Earth Realm.

III

Resolve

Win — or be knocked out of the astral plane.

Level Design

The Astral Plane

A galactic, flowing cosmic corridor thick with drifting star-matter, luminous nebulae, and soul-currents. Movement here feels weightless and fluid — a suspension between worlds.

Far off in the distance stands a destructible crystalline structure. The player must reach and destroy it to complete the Gameplay Demo.

Technical Strategy

The guiding constraint for Empyrean Rifts is budget: the demo is built entirely on existing subscriptions and local hardware, at zero additional cost. Every agent in Section 06 is routed to a platform already covered by a standing plan or to a machine already on hand — so the marginal cash cost of producing this demo is effectively $0. The strategy below maps the token budget onto real platforms and identifies the hard technical ceiling.

Claude Team (Pro)

Claude Code & Claude Design

A Pro-level Claude Team account covers every reasoning-heavy agent — Architect, design, gameplay, animation, audio, marketing, and QA. Claude Code drives UE5.8 through MCP; Claude Design owns UI and asset specifications. Budget is spent as included seat usage rather than per-token cash.

Google Pro

Antigravity Platform

A Google Pro account provides access to the Antigravity agentic development platform, running the Gemini-backed implementation agents (UE Level Builder, Menu and HUD Designers, UMG Engineer). This offloads high-volume, lower-reasoning build work onto a second standing subscription.

Local Orchestration

Hermes Agentic Framework

A locally-hosted Hermes agentic framework coordinates the multi-agent pipeline — passing documents, specifications, and error reports between agents — so orchestration runs on local hardware with no recurring service fee.

Local GPU

Hunyuan3D on RTX 5070

All 3D asset generation runs locally on a single RTX 5070, eliminating cloud GPU costs entirely. Hunyuan3D builds base meshes at ~6 GB VRAM and generates textures at ~12 GB — both within the card's budget.

Key Constraint · VRAM Ceiling

The RTX 5070's VRAM is the binding technical constraint. Texture generation (~12 GB) sits at the top of the card's usable budget, so 3D generation is run sequentially, one stage at a time — meshing first, then texturing — rather than in parallel. Batching this way avoids out-of-memory failures and keeps the entire asset pipeline on local hardware, preserving the zero-cash-cost goal at the cost of longer wall-clock generation time.
